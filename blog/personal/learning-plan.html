Learning Plan

09 September 2021

What is my long term goal and/or career pathway?
I want a good work/life balance - whether that's the ability to wfh or shift my working hours when possible. Even more than this I want to work in a field that I'm proud of and interested in. I have interests in gaming, space (hello rocket lab), film, robotics/automation but I'd be happy doing anything that lets you get into the nitty gritty of how something works.

My strengths and limitations in relation to learning
Strengths:

  • Problem solving - I'll work at something until I get it - it's my favourite type of endorphin!
  • Glass half full - I try to see the positive in every scenario even when it feels like everythings broken.
  • Focus - when I get in a flow I'll just keep going with full focus.
  • I love learning - it's insatiable and I always want to know how to do everything I can.
  • I don't just want to know how to do something, I want to know the best way.

Limitations:
  • My love of solving problems will sometimes make me too independant - I can get stuck on a problem for hours because I want the satisfaction of solving it myself.
  • Focus - I can get stuck in my focused state and become time blind - I'll work on something for hours when I should really take a break.
  • If what I've made doesn't match my vision I can feel like my creation is a failure.
  • Confidence - I always think about my limitations more than my achievements.
  • Sometimes I'll try to learn too much at once too fast.
  • If there's too many environment inputs (certain visuals/sounds) and I'm already tired from masking, if something else happens (unexpected schedule changes is one I can struggle with) I can hit an asd overload and need to give myself time to recover/recharge. This can be tough when I just want to keep working.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would I like to see developed in myself while at Dev Academy?

  • Collaboration and networking - I want more confidence talking to people I don't know and asking for help.
  • I want to learn to back myself and stop selling myself short - I don't like to feel like I'm bragging and I'm not great at negotiating for myself/my skills.

My commandments to how I will manage my workload in this programme
I will stay focused when working - asking for help when needed but also giving help to others when able.
When things start to get to me I will take a break - whether it's to go for a walk outside, meditating somewhere quiet or to listen to music with noise cancelling. I will ask for help if it's relevant to the situation or if I am overwhelmed for an extended period.
I will set a time limit on myself when I hit a problem (is it a small line of code? set 15mins. Is it a large section to understand? set 60mins with a break midway). If I exceed my own time limit I will ask my peers to see if they've encountered the same roadblock/if they can help. Otherwise I will reach out to my facillitator/another facillitator if it's more complex and I need more detail.
I expect the facilitation team to have compassion if something has really got me down/stuck and to call me out if they notice me spending too much time on something I shouldn't be.
I don't have any fixed commitments but I like to take an exercise break during the day based on when I need time away from the screen.
