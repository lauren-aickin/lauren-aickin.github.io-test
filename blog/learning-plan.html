09 September 2021

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?
I want a good work/life balance - whether that's the ability to wfh or shift my working hours (eg. 4 x 10 hour days for 3 days off). Even more than this I want to work in a field that I'm proud of and interested in. I have interests in gaming, space (hello rocket lab), film, robotics/ automation but I'd be happy doing anything that lets you get into the nitty gritty of how something works.

A description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning
Strengths:
Problem solving - I'll work at something until I get it - it's my favourite type of endorphin! Glass half full - I try to see the positive in every scenario even when it feels like everythings broken. Focus - when I get in a flow I'll just keep going with full focus. I love learning - it's insatiable and I always want to know how to do everything I can. I don't just want to know how to do something, I want to know the best way.

Limitations:
My love of solving problems will sometimes make me too independant - I can get stuck on a problem for hours because I want the satisfaction of solving it myself. Focus - I can get stuck in my focused state and become time blind - I'll work on something for hours when I should really take a break. If what I've made doesn't match my vision I can feel like my creation is a failure. Confidence - I always think about my limitations more than my achievements. Sometimes I'll try to learn too much at once too fast. Sometimes if there's already too many environment inputs (visual/sound) and I'm already tired from masking, if something else small happens (unexpected schedule changes) I can hit an asd overload.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?
Collaboration and networking - I want more confidence talking to people I don't know and asking for help. Stop selling myself short/interview skills - I don't like to feel like I'm bragging and I'm not good at negotiating for myself/my skills.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include:
An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives
Stay focused when working - ask for help when needed but also give help to others when able.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you
Take a break - go for a walk outside somewhere quiet/meditate/listen to music with noise cancelling. Ask for help if it's relevant to the situation.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way
Set a time limit on myself when I hit a problem (is it a small line of code? set 15mins. Is it a large section to understand? set 60mins with a break midway) Ask my peers initially - see if they've encountered the same roadblock/if they can help. Reach out to my facillitator/another facillitator if the above doesn't work, it's more complex and I need more detail.

A description of what you expect from the facilitation team
Compassion if something has really got me down/stuck. Call me out if you notice me spending too much time on something I shouldn't be.
